simple style -オヒルノオト-

Original Date: December 2015
An interview on JFN's simple style －オヒルノオト - (Midday Report) radio program, conducted by its host Maiko Itai.

Good afternoon.

Lamp

Lamp

Someya

Nagai

Sakakibara

I'm Kaori Sakakibara, the vocalist.

Itai

They're a group that has been well-received by music enthusiasts for their delicate and high-quality melodies and sounds. Last year, they started their own label, Botanical House. You released with KIDSAREDEAD on there, and when he appeared on simple style the other day, Someya, you came as well.

Someya

Yes, that's right. I went inside the studio, but I left without saying a word. laughs

Nagai

I chose it.

Nagai

That photo...

This one!

Nagai

..."A Fun Tour". laughs

Nagai

Despite my voice, it actually was really fun.

Nagai

This is Fukuoka, we did a tour with KIDSAREDEAD. This was the first time we've done this kind of tour in Japan.

Itai

That’s true, you guys typically go to very specific places.

Nagai

So, we all traveled together. After the show, we had dinner together, just like any other band would do. It was our first time doing that, and it was really enjoyable.

Itai

Great, wasn't it?

Nagai

It was all people who I enjoy together, so it was fun.

Itai

I hope you can see Nagai's big smile. Thank you very much. That's all for one frame of Nagai, one frame of Lamp. Now, I would like to ask the members of Lamp to perform a live studio show for us as the first part of our special. May I ask you to standby?

Someya

I'm getting nervous! laughs

Itai

I'm already looking forward to it! Can you tell me a bit about the setup of today's performance?

Someya

I, Someya, will play electric guitar, Nagai will sing vocals and play acoustic guitar, Sakakibara will sing vocals and play the synthesizer depending on the song. She'll also play percussion, instruments like the shaker.

Itai

In your normal live performances, you often have support players, right?

Someya

We almost never perform with only the three of us.

Itai

So it's quite rare. Yes, please enjoy it. What's the first song that you're going to play?

Someya

We'll perform the song "Yume Utsutsu", which is the only song that we've recorded that was done by just the three of us. All of our other songs have other people contributing in them, but I think this one was us alone.

Itai

So today we can hear it as it was recorded. simple style Christmas edition, by Lamp—live.

—Lamp performs "Yume Utsutsu"—
Itai

Thank you very much.

Lamp

Thank you.

Itai

I think one of the lyrics of the song is "light blue afternoon", which is this time of the day. When I listened to it, I felt like throwing everything away and dozing off. laughs

Sakakibara

It's a good feeling. laughs

Itai

So, today, we have a Christmas edition of simple style with the three members of Lamp. This portion of the show is called "Lunch Notes", where we ask our guests what they'd like to share for the listeners to take note of. Kaori, could you tell us a little bit about yourself?

Sakakibara

Sure.

Itai

Let's hear it.

Sakakibara

Today, I'd like to tell you about an exhibit.

Itai

Information on an exhibit?

Sakakibara

It's at the Yayoi Museum, in Yanaka.

Itai

Yes—in Yanaka, Tokyo.

Sakakibara

It's a museum for well-known artists. There's a permanent exhibit, where there's currently a special showcase of original drawings by my favourite manga artist, Ako Mutsu.

Itai

How long will it be on display for at the museum?

Sakakibara

Until the 25th of December, Christmas Day.

Itai

"Ako Mutsu" (陸奥 A子) is written as "Riku" (陸) and "Oku" (奥), am I right? I'm of that generation, but it was quite a while ago.

Sakakibara

Yes, in the 70's and 80's, she did a series in “Ribon”, or what was it then? It was a kind of otome manga, and I heard that it took the world by storm. When I was in elementary school, at the back of the Ribon mascot comics, there was a thing where various artists were being introduced with a showcase of their work.

Itai

There was.

Sakakibara

That's right. At the time I was reading it, it wasn't being serialised, so I didn't know about it, but there was a list, like a back number on there.

Itai

It was published as a book, or rather, it was a partial book.

Sakakibara

There's a section with about six of Ako Mutsu's titles in it, and they're really tiny, about three centimetres across. I really love the titles there, they're all very nice.

Itai

What are some of them, for example?

Sakakibara

For example, "Sukoshi Dake Kataomoi" and "Kinou Mita Yume". There was also one called "Tameiki No Yukue". I love that title so much.

Someya

It's on the Lamp album "Yume".

Sakakibara

I used it.

Itai

The “Yukue”?

Sakakibara

I really loved it when I read it, it's like poetry. There's something very sentimental about it that appeals to my emotions.

Itai

Wow. Ako Mutsu, again, for those of you who are listening to this now, hearing it in real time, but the “A” is the (Latin) letter “A”, right?

Sakakibara

The fact that she's called "Ako" with the character “A” is mysterious, and I thought it was nice.

Itai

What do you think of manga? Do you two read them as well?

Someya

I read those comics too, sometimes. I like the old comics.

Itai

Which writers?

Someya

I like Shigeru Mizuki, Daijiro Moroboshi, Yoshiharu Tsuge.

Itai

Refined taste.

Someya

I also like the manga of Seiichi Hayashi.

Nagai

You also like Hazuki Oji, right?

Someya

Yes.

Itai

Oh, and you too, Nagai?

Nagai

Well, I was also influenced by Someya-senpai.

Itai

"Senpai"? laughs

Nagai

Because they both like manga very much. What I particularly like is the world of Yoshiharu Tsuge. When I was a university student, I borrowed a copy and read it, and at the time—I think there are all sorts of different types of manga, but there's the kind that's more like art, and then there's the kind where Yoshiharu Tsuge himself appears. I like it because it's really interesting, and the images of Japan that appear in it, which we don't have much of now, are really something. It's like the image of Japan from the 70's. Maybe it's something like "Waiting for the Wind" from Happy End. I'm really attracted to that kind of image of Japan that doesn't exist anymore. When we were forming the band—how should I put it...?

Sakakibara

When we got together, we only read manga.

Itai

Is that so?

Sakakibara

In the beginning.

Itai

In the beginning?

Sakakibara

Yes.

Itai

So you all chose what you wanted to read?

Someya

We'd come to my house and pick from what I had, and we'd read the entire time, without practicing. We'd end up just having dinner. laughs

Itai

So that's how Lamp started. laughs

Someya

Of what we saw in what we read, there was quite a bit of the type of worldview that we felt together. We would try to get that atmosphere in the pictures we take of the band. So we'd go to the countryside in Japan and look for that kind of atmosphere and go to different places and take pictures. There's something about that.

Itai

So that world is quite an inspiration for you all, isn't it? They've just named their favorite comic writers, did you get the memo?
